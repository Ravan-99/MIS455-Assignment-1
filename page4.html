Fish Species

The ocean is home to an incredible variety of fish species, each playing a vital role in the ecosystem. From large predators like tuna and sharks to small schooling fish like sardines, the diversity of marine life is stunning.

Fishermen often target specific species depending on the season and demand. Some of the most popular fish species include cod, haddock, salmon, and mackerel. These species are not only valuable for their nutritional content but also support millions of jobs around the world.

In undivided India, fishermen were most numerous in Bengal, followed by the Madras presidency; in the 1930s, these two provinces respectively accounted for 32 and 28 percent of the total fishermen in India. According to the census of 1941, Bengal had a total of five hundred thousand fishermen, and nearly three hundred thousand people were engaged in selling fish in hats and bazaars. Overall, 1.6 percent of the total population of Bengal lived on fishing and associated vocations. The percentage was higher in Dhaka, Rajshahi, and Presidency divisions, standing at 2.6 percent.

Bangladesh is home to some of the most popular freshwater and coastal fish species in South Asia. The Hilsa (Tenualosa ilisha), Bangladesh’s national fish, is particularly celebrated. Known for its tender, flavorful flesh, it is central to many traditional dishes and holds cultural significance, especially during celebrations. Other species like Ruhi (Labeo rohita), Catla (Catla catla), and Pabda (Ompok pabda) are popular for their taste and nutritional value. Each of these species contributes to the country’s biodiversity and plays an essential role in the country’s cuisine.
